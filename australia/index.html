Value of Weight Loss - Australia

Welcome to the home page of the Australian adaptation of the Value of Weight Loss Simulation Model. This model is based on a real-world study1 that utilized data from electronic medical records to estimate the risk of developing ten different obesity-related complications (ORCs).

The Value of Weight Loss Simulation Model uses a risk engine to estimate the future clinical and economic burden of obesity for Australia, based on population demographics and the local direct treatment costs for the ten ORCs.

The web interface for the Value of Weight Loss Simulation Model allows users to assess the potential clinical and economic implications for different scenarios (no intervention, weight loss, weight gain) and sub-cohorts that are representative of the population living with obesity in Australia. Two different web interfaces are available, one focusing on cohort-level simulation and the other on population-level simulation.

The cohort-based simulation is conducted using a random cohort that is representative of the Australian population, based on the selected gender, BMI, and age ranges. Whenever the sliders are adjusted to change the inclusion ranges, a new cohort based on the selected criteria will be used for the simulation. A typical use case for this version of the simulation is to examine the effect of treating 100,000 individuals from the Australian population with a BMI ranging from 30 to 50.

The population-based simulation is conducted on a subset of all individuals from the full Australian population with the selected gender, BMI, and age ranges. In contrast to the cohort-based version, when the ranges for BMI are narrowed, the population subset is reduced to the number of individuals in the full population that have exactly the selected criteria.
